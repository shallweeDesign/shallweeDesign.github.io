About Kerry Hill

Kerry Hill (1943–2018) was an Australian master architect who became one of Asia's most celebrated designers of luxury resort hotels from the 1970s onwards. He founded Kerry Hill Architects in Singapore and his work spans Southeast Asia, Australia, Japan and the United States.

Kerry Hill's architectural philosophy can be summed up in one line: architecture is not about conquering nature, but about conversing with the land. His designs captivate not through grandeur, but through quiet strength. Using the simplest lines and most natural materials, he created spaces of profound depth — allowing light, water and wind to flow through each site so that buildings could be felt, not merely seen.

He coined the concept of "Ongoing Style" — architecture that does not age with the years but instead leads its era forward. Great buildings, he believed, should only grow more timeless.

The Lalu Sun Moon Lake is one of the most important expressions of Kerry Hill's aesthetic vision. Through Zen minimalism, natural timber, stone and glass curtain walls, he wove the building into the lake's landscape. A decade later, Kerry Hill collaborated again on The Lalu Qingdao — continuing his signature style by drawing inspiration from shipping containers and integrating the sea view with an infinity mirror pool.
